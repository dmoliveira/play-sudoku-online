❓ FAQ · よくある質問

Free Sudoku online, from easy to expert

How do I play Sudoku?

Fill the grid so every row, column, and 3×3 box contains the numbers 1 to 9 exactly once. Start with easy boards, scan for missing numbers, and use notes when needed.

Read the full beginner guide ↗

Which mode should I choose?

Classic is the balanced default. Zen is calmer with notes-friendly play. Sprint is for speed. No mistakes rejects wrong entries instantly. Daily puzzle gives a shared challenge at your chosen difficulty.

What difficulty should I start with?

Easy is best for beginners. Medium adds more candidate work. Hard rewards structure and patience. Expert is best when you want a deeper, slower challenge.

Why does this site feel different?

Sudoku Sakura is designed to keep the board large and readable while still supporting repeat play with progress, rank, daily rhythm, and optional guidance tools.

Can I play Sudoku on mobile?

Yes. The layout is responsive and the number pad is touch friendly.

Can I hide wrong-guess feedback?

Yes. Turn off “Show wrong guesses” for a more traditional challenge, then use “Check board” only when you want explicit feedback.

Does the site save my best times?

Yes. Best times, solve counts, starts, streaks, and other local engagement stats are stored in your browser.

What is the daily puzzle?

The daily mode picks a deterministic puzzle for the current date and difficulty, so every player gets the same challenge that day.

What difficulty should beginners start with?

Start with Easy, then move to Medium once scanning rows, columns, and boxes feels natural.

Why play free Sudoku online here?

Sudoku Sakura focuses on a play-first layout with elegant visuals, challenge modes, local progress, and fast ways back into the next session instead of burying the game under too much copy.

How can I improve over time?

Use the guide pages, try daily puzzles, and move up through the rank and achievement systems gradually. Consistency helps more than rushing.
